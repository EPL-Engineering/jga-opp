MOTU setup

 

 

 

 

MOTU Monitor 8

 

The MOTU Monitor 8 functions as a combination USB sound card and signal router/mixer. Note that the MOTU requires several seconds to boot and it should be started before launching the software. Front and back views are shown below. Most of the connections are on the back of the device.

 

Network connection

The MOTU is connected to the PC via a direct Ethernet connection. It is probably simplest to use a USB-to-Ethernet dongle on the PC for this purpose. In this case, the USB-to-Ethernet adapter must be set to obtain an IP address automatically (which will start with 169.254.xxx.xxx)

USB connection

The MOTU is also connected to the PC via a USB cable. Note that this has only seemed to work when plugged into the back of the PC

Participant

The participant audio comes from monitor group C. Each monitor group has four 1/4" outputs available. The one in the top left is a standard stereo headphone jack. The two on the right, labeled "L" and "R" split the output into two mono signals, appropriate for send audio to insert earphones. The 1/4" jack on the bottom left is a summed mono output.

Waver

The waver audio comes from monitor group B. The stereo headphone jack in the upper left is the appropriate choice.

Caregiver

The caregiver audio comes from monitor group A. The stereo headphone jack in the upper left is the appropriate choice.

Tester

The tester can listen to the waver by connecting headphones to channel E, which is probably most convenient on the front of the MOTU device.

AcousticPTC

Participant audio for standard method PTC testing comes from monitor group D. The one in the top left is a standard stereo headphone jack. The two on the right, labeled "L" and "R" split the output into two mono signals, appropriate for send audio to insert earphones.
