OPP Overview

 

 

 

 

Overview

 

The diagram below summarizes the people and equipment involved in the OPP experiment.

 

 

Tester

The tester controls the experiment on a PC from outside the sound booth.

MOTU sound card/mixer

The MOTU device in the rack is a combination USB sound card and real-time audio router and mixer. Sounds are generated on the PC are routed through the MOTU to the human listeners inside the booth.

Waver

The waver is the investigator's assistant inside the booth. Generally, they listen over headphones to a "background" signal meant to prevent them from detecting when the target stimulus is presented to the participant. The waver is also able to hear the investigator via a USB mic attached to the PC. During the Training phase, the waver hears the same signal as the Participant so that they can help the Participant get engaged in the task.

USB microphones

The USB microphones allow the tester to talk to the waver inside the booth and vice versa. The microphone signals are sent from the PC to the MOTU. The signal sent to the wave is also mixed with the background sound.

Caregiver

The participant's caregiver listens to the same "background" signal as the waver, to prevent them from detecting when the target stimulus is presented to the participant.

Participant

The participant listens to the test stimulus over a freefield speaker. They may also hear the sound from a video shown to them as a reward for a correct response.

Toys

The MVRA toys are controlled from the PC via a USB connection to the Ontrak controller

Ontrak

The Ontrak controller generates the digital signals to individually control each of the MVRA toys and the motor. There is a custom-built adapter that connects the Ontrak output to a 9-pin DIN connector compatible with the MVRA hand controller input.

Video

Videos are shown using the programmatically-controllable VLC media player on the PC.
